# Visa & Tax Processing Agents

## Transparent Automation for Government Services

A major EU government agency deployed autonomous visa-decision and tax-refund agents serving hundreds of thousands of citizens. Requirement: every agent decision must be auditable, explainable, and compliant with the EU AI Act—especially transparency obligations for citizen-facing decisions.

### The Challenge

Visa processing (traditional):
- 500K applications/year
- Manual review: 2–6 weeks per application
- Multiple stages: eligibility screening → risk assessment → interviewer review
- Cost: €40 per application (€20M annual cost)

Tax refund processing (traditional):
- 2M applications/year
- Manual verification: verify income docs → cross-check tax records → calculate refund
- Scope for error: ~3% false positives (wrong refunds sent)
- Cost: €15 per application (€30M annual cost)

**New requirement:** EU AI Act mandates that citizen-facing high-risk AI decisions must be:
- **Transparent:** Citizens can understand why the decision was made
- **Traceable:** Full audit trail available on request
- **Contestable:** Citizens must have appeal mechanism with human review
- **Logged:** Every decision documented with reasoning

### EU AI Act Compliance

**Regulation → How Kakunin Satisfies It**

| Article | Requirement | Kakunin Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 13** | Transparency for high-risk decisions | Agent signs every decision + immutable audit log |
| **Art. 14** | Human oversight capability | Appeal path: citizen → human supervisor (reviews full log) |
| **Art. 15** | Robustness & security | Agent cert revoked if risk score drifts |
| **Art. 22 (GDPR)** | Right to explanation for automated decisions | Citizen portal shows full audit trail + risk score breakdown |
| **Art. 30 (GDPR)** | Records of processing (RoPA) | Audit log is RoPA (signed, timestamped, tamper-evident) |

**Regulator audit result:** "This system transparently shows citizens why they were approved/denied. Full audit trail is available. Appeal mechanism has human review. Compliant with Art. 13–15."
